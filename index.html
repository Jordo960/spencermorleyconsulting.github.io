Nearly half of your staff are probably already using AI. Half of them are using publicly available tools — tools that your ratepayers' personal information should never touch. This is called Shadow AI, and it is a live compliance risk in your municipality right now.

Every province has privacy legislation governing how municipalities collect, use, and disclose personal information. In Alberta, the new Access to Information Act (ATIA) and Protection of Privacy Act (POPA) require every public body to have a formal Privacy Management Program in place by June 11, 2026 — including documented AI use policies, designated privacy officers, and staff training. Other provinces operate under equivalent legislation: Ontario's MFIPPA, BC's FOIPPA, Manitoba's FIPPA, and others. The specifics differ. The direction is the same: unmanaged AI use is a liability, and leadership policy is the single most impactful lever available.

SMC's four-stage framework closes the Shadow AI gap and builds an AI capability at the same time — so your municipality ends up both compliant and more efficient, not just one or the other.

Before spending a dollar on AI — and before your next privacy audit — you need to know where you actually stand. Then we identify where AI can save your team the most time, and deliver a plain-language roadmap your council can understand and approve. Delivered remotely, with minimal demands on your team's time.

02
Staff AI Training & Prompt Engineering
A staff member who enters ratepayer personal information into a public AI tool isn't just making a mistake — they may be triggering a privacy breach under your province's legislation. And with 85% of Canadians already lacking confidence in how the public service uses AI, one incident can become a council issue fast.
But compliance is only half the training story. The quality of what your staff get out of AI depends entirely on the quality of what they put in. Prompt engineering — the skill of instructing AI clearly and effectively — is what separates a useful tool from a frustrating one. SMC's training covers both:
Compliance & Safe Use
What information must never enter a public AI tool. How to identify a Shadow AI risk. Privacy-by-default practices for public sector work. Disclosure requirements and when to keep a human in the loop before AI output reaches the public.
Prompt Engineering for Municipal Work
Hands-on techniques for common municipal tasks — drafting council communications, summarizing meeting notes, responding to ratepayer inquiries, preparing reports. Includes chain-of-thought and prompt chaining methods for complex analytical work, and practices for requesting inclusive, bias-aware outputs.
Critical Evaluation of AI Output
AI can hallucinate — generating confident-sounding information that is factually wrong. Staff learn to structure prompts to minimize this risk, use explicit examples to anchor AI responses, and apply a consistent review process before any AI-generated content is used in public-facing contexts.
Available remotely or on-site. No jargon. Skills your team uses the next day.

02
WebMCP Readiness Assessment
If you've invested in SEO, you already understand why being found matters. WebMCP — Model Context Protocol — is the next stage of that. It's the emerging standard that lets AI platforms like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity read, understand, and recommend your business directly in conversation.
When someone asks ChatGPT "who's the best plumber in Lethbridge" or "where should I eat in Red Deer tonight," the businesses that show up aren't the ones with the most Google reviews — they're the ones whose digital presence is structured for AI to read. WebMCP is how you get there.
This matters because AI recommendations carry a different kind of weight with consumers. Unlike a sponsored search result or a boosted post, a recommendation from an AI assistant is perceived as independent and genuine — which means it converts differently. SMC's WebMCP Readiness Assessment evaluates your current digital presence, identifies what needs to change, and maps out exactly what to implement to make your business visible and recommendable to AI platforms before your competitors get there. WebMCP only became available in February 2026. The window to move first is open right now.

Staff AI Training & Prompt Engineering
The biggest reason AI tools don't stick in small businesses isn't resistance — it's that nobody was shown how to use them well. A staff member who gets a mediocre result from AI once or twice concludes that AI isn't useful. The problem is almost always the prompt, not the tool.
SMC's training is hands-on and built around your team's actual job functions — customer communications, scheduling, content creation, quoting, reporting, whatever eats the most time. Staff learn how to get genuinely useful output from AI tools on the first try: how to structure a prompt, how to give the AI the right context, how to critically evaluate what comes back, and how to handle customer information safely.
